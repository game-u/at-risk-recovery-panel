Recommendations

Family cancels are the largest source of unrealized revenue (~$893,694 gross). Makeups already win back ~24% ($211,866); the remaining $681,828 net can only be recovered through retention outreach and behavior change — the biggest lever in the plan. A realistic 12-month recovery (25–40% of the net) is $170,457–$272,731/yr.
1. Family cancels — $681,828 net
RETENTION · #1 lever · target $170K–$273K
  • Auto-flag families at 3+ cancels in 30 days OR attendance below 70%. The Top 25 Heavy-Cancel Families list above is the current queue.
  • Frame the conversation as break-vs-churn: offer a structured 4- or 8-week pause with a re-engagement plan instead of letting families silently drift out.
  • Personalized coordinator outreach beats email — especially for high-value families with 20+ cancels YTD.
  • Track: monthly family-cancel count trend + next-class-attendance rate after outreach.
2. Instructor callouts — $119,027 residual
Supply-side · lift callout coverage (now ~60%)
  • Smaller than the family bucket, but not zero — we cover ~60% of last-minute callouts with a sub YTD; the ~40% we can't is this residual. Advance-notice PTO is covered ~86%.
  • Continue sub premium reform + thin-bench recruiting to trim the residual net exposure.
  • Finish Scheduling Bots rollout to shrink acceptance latency.
  • Track: emergency answer rate (already in the CX dashboard).
3. Unclassified — $42,054
Data hygiene · target $50–80K after reclassification
  • Standardize override reasons in XP as a small dropdown (Family Absence, Family No-Show, Instructor Out, Game-U Closure, etc.). Free-text is what created the bare-“Absence” misclassification bug that hid family cancels for months.
  • Once tagged, this bucket collapses — most will reclassify to Family.
  • Add a required reason field so this bucket never grows past a small residual.
4. Game-U closures — ~$0 net
Workflow check · small bucket
  • Mostly holiday closures — families get a reschedule offer, most makeups get taught and billed.
  • Small residual of truly discretionary Game-U cancels; low priority.
Cross-cutting: how these connect to the Break study
The Top 25 heaviest cancel families are the pre-break pipeline. Break Study shows ~80% of formal-break students never return — so intercepting families BEFORE the formal break is filed is the highest-leverage move in the whole plan. The retention playbook for bucket #1 is the front-end of Break-study prevention.
Ownership & cadence: Brief refreshes daily. Top 25 family list divided among coordinators for outreach each Monday; results reported at the following huddle. Track cumulative $ recovered against target quarterly.
